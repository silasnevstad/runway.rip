### 1. Choose a Hosting Provider

You can deploy your app to any hosting provider that supports Next.js. Some popular options include:
- [AWS](https://aws.amazon.com/)
- [Vercel](https://vercel.com/)
- [Fly.io](https://fly.io)
- [Railway](https://railway.com)
- [Render](https://render.com/)
- [DigitalOcean](https://www.digitalocean.com/solutions/nextjs-hosting)
- [GitHub Pages](https://pages.github.com/)
- [Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/run)

### 2. Configure Your Hosting Provider
Each hosting provider has its own configuration process. Here are some general steps:

- Create a new project on your hosting provider.
- Connect your Git repository to the project.
- Configure environment variables in your hosting provider's dashboard. Make sure to include all the variables from your `.env.local` file that you use.

<Caution>
    Do not forgot to configure your environment variables in your hosting provider's dashboard, `.env.local` is not used in production.
</Caution>

<div id="checklists" />
### 3. Deployment Checklist
Before deploying your app, make sure to check the following:

- **Using auth?** Follow the [Auth](/docs/features/auth) tutorial and configured your auth provider.
- **Using payments?** Follow the [Payments](/docs/features/payments) tutorial and configured your payment provider.
- **Need Policies?** Use the [Policies](/docs/features/policies) guide to create your policies.


### 4. Deploy Your App
Launch your app and make sure everything is working as expected!
